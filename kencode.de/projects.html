The world's first "Shopping Cart" software

Line9 Corp
Sounds hard to believe, but yes, ken invented the online Shopping Cart "app" in 1993. Google "tek9.asp" and you will see over 70,000 websites still using ken's invention even today (over 20 years later!). His Oregon company at that time was entitled "Line9 LLC" (what was Line9.com). Amazon then tried enforcing their new "one click checkout" patent in 1998 and threatened ken (Line9 Corp) with a massive lawsuit if he did not stop selling his Tek9 Pro eBusiness Software immediately. When ken provided proof of use in business and his Notary dated inventions portfolio, Amazon backed off. A proud day it was to say the least.

Acquisition offers were made for Line9 Corp, but none were acceptable. Thank god for NDA/NCA's! Line9 business boomed for over 16 years and at its peak retained a distributed team of 37 employees and sub-contractors worldwide (primarily in India, Ukraine, Latvia and Romania).

The world's first "Smart Car"

Smart Car
In 1989, ken created what he believes to be the first "smart" car. This car (see photo to the right) was a 1989 Nissan Sentra. ken removed the door handles and locks, filled the car with relays, sensors, solenoids, servo motors and custom programmed the Clifford alarm system himself. When the car got too hot, it would automatically crack the windows. The programmable remote control allowed ken to open the doors remotely, open the trunk, crack the sunroof open, start the engine, pre-warm the seats, roll the windows up and down, lock/unlock the doors and of course arm/disarm the alarm. Keep in mind, most of those options were not available from any auto manufacturers yet. Even today, you would be hard pressed to find a car that will open the door for you or automatically crack the windows on a hot day.
